WHERE IT'S MADE
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Central Minnesota. A long way from the places that name themselves the future.

The maker spent three decades as a software architect — from the first web to
the latest wave, building systems for companies across the world, watching an
industry mistake complexity for progress and novelty for meaning.

The promise of technology was always larger than what arrived. That it might
close the distance between people. That it might make the world feel more alive.

That promise, mostly, went unkept.

MemoryCharm is a different kind of working.

The technology is here — but it is meant to disappear. What remains is the
moment: a tap, a memory, a thing held in the hand.

No trends pursued. No fads chased. No features added for their own sake.

Only the quiet conviction that the world deserves more magic.
And that we are capable of making it.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- The closing two lines are meant to feel earned, not proclaimed.
